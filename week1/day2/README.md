# Day 2 – SQL: GROUP BY, Aggregations & Joins

## Overview

On Day 2 of the training, I focused on strengthening my SQL skills by working on **GROUP BY operations, advanced aggregation functions, and different types of joins**. This helped me understand how to analyze and combine data from multiple tables.

## What I Learned

* Fundamentals of **GROUP BY** in SQL
* Use of aggregation functions like:

  * `SUM()`
  * `COUNT()`
  * `AVG()`
  * `MAX()`
  * `MIN()`
* Advanced GROUP BY concepts using:

  * `HAVING` clause
  * Conditional filtering on aggregated data
* Different types of **joins**:

  * INNER JOIN
  * LEFT JOIN
  * RIGHT JOIN
  * FULL OUTER JOIN

## Practical Tasks Performed

* Solved multiple SQL queries on employee and sales datasets
* Calculated:

  * Total salary per department
  * Average salary and employee count
  * Maximum and minimum values per group
* Applied **HAVING clause** to filter grouped results
* Performed joins between tables to:

  * Calculate total sales per employee
  * Analyze product-wise sales
  * Combine employee and department data
* Practiced real-world scenarios like:

  * Finding top-performing employees
  * Identifying employees with no sales
  * Analyzing department-level performance

## Key Concepts Practiced

* GROUP BY with aggregation
* GROUP BY with HAVING
* GROUP BY with joins
* Multi-table joins
* Real-world query scenarios

## Challenges Faced

* Understanding when to use `WHERE` vs `HAVING`.
* Writing correct join conditions between tables
* Handling NULL values in joins
* Debugging aggregation errors

## Conclusion

Day 2 helped me build a strong foundation in SQL by practicing grouping, aggregations, and joins. These concepts are essential for analyzing structured data and are widely used in real-world data engineering tasks.
